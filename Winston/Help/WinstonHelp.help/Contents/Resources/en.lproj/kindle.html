Sending books to a Kindle

Connecting

  • Connect your Kindle with a USB cable. Winston detects modern Kindles over MTP and older ones that mount as a USB drive.
  • When connected, a green device entry appears in the sidebar under “Device”. Click it to see what's on the Kindle.

Sending & importing

  • Send specific books: select them in your library and click Send to Device in the toolbar.
  • Or use the two buttons in the device view's header:
    • Review Sync Plan previews every change before it happens: add, update an outdated conversion, repair a cover, keep unchanged, or optionally remove a device-only copy. Removals are off by default and require confirmation.
    • ⬇ Import N to Library copies books that are on the Kindle but not yet in your library into your library.
  • Each Kindle gets its own local sync profile. Use the profile menu in the plan to switch, create, or rename profiles; Winston remembers the source file and cover last sent to each one.
  • Transfers run one at a time. Progress (including conversion) shows in a status toast in the bottom‑right corner, with a Cancel button to stop the queue.
  • Books already on the device are marked with a badge in your library.

Managing what's on the Kindle

  • The device view lists what's on the Kindle, with each book's author (matched from your library).
  • Right‑click to Copy to Library or Remove from Device — singly or for a multi‑selection.
  • To clear out an author, right‑click a book by them and choose Remove all by ⟨author⟩.

Highlights & notes

  • Click the 💬 button in the device header to import the highlights and notes from the Kindle's clippings file.
  • They're matched to the matching books in your library; browse and export them later from Library → Highlights… (see Highlights).

Converting for Kindle

  • Kindles can't read sideloaded EPUBs, so Winston automatically creates a Kindle-friendly MOBI or AZW3 copy when sending.
  • You can also convert ahead of time from the context menu, Book menu, or inspector. The generated format is kept beside the source, so Winston can detect when it becomes outdated.
  • Winston converts common formats to MOBI natively. Other conversions use calibre and its ebook-convert tool.
  • Winston also pushes a cover thumbnail to the Kindle so sideloaded books show their artwork (best effort; depends on the device firmware).

Themes & settings

  • Press ⌘⇧T to cycle themes, or choose one in Settings → Appearance: Purple (retro), White, and Black (classic dark).
  • All themes share the same native macOS layout — only colors, fonts, and wording change.
  • Switch the app language (English / Czech) in Settings → Language; it applies after a relaunch.
  • Other preferences live in Settings (⌘,): online metadata, a Hardcover token for community ratings, a watch folder for auto‑import, and automatic backup.
